Colorimetric observer models¶

Alphaopic irradiance, the ammount of incident light that is ‘seen’ by individual photoreceptor classes, is a core computation for silent substitution. It is calculated by weighting a light spectrum by estimates of the photoreceptor spectral sensetivites via a simple dot product operation.

The International Commission on Illumination (CIE) define average colorimetric observer models (e.g., CIE 1931 Standard Colorimetric Observer), which include estimates of the photoreceptor spectral sensetivities, assuming a stimulus of a certain size. The UCL Colour Vision Research Laboratory maintain a repository of these data.

pysilsub.observers.StandardColorimetricObserver¶

This is the standard observer model for 10-degree field size.

[2]:

from pysilsub import observers

observer = observers.StandardColorimetricObserver()
observer.plot_action_spectra()

pysilsub.observers.IndividualColorimetricObserver¶

In reality, the exact shape of photoreceptor spectral sensetivities varies between individuals due to differences in ocular physiology. Prereceptoral filtering by the lens and other ocular media plays a big part in this.

It is possible to adjust the spectral sensetivity curves to account for lens density and muclar pigment.

[3]:

observer = observers.IndividualColorimetricObserver(age=60, field_size=10)
observer.plot_action_spectra()
